# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Compliance and risk officers at a large organization, working at a desk during focused sessions. Their primary job is reviewing third-party due diligence submissions, approving or declining vendors, managing risk exposure, and configuring the compliance workflow. They work methodically — opening profiles, reading documents, comparing risk signals, triggering processes — not glancing at dashboards intermittently.

## Product Purpose

A Third-Party Risk Management (RCTP) platform for onboarding, vetting, and monitoring third parties (vendors, contractors, entities, persons). Manages the full lifecycle: intake, duplicate checking, entity verification, risk scoring, approval workflows, renewals, and role-based access control. Success means a compliance officer can make confident risk decisions faster, with less manual process overhead.

## Brand Personality

Professional but approachable. The platform takes its job seriously — compliance work has real stakes — but should never feel bureaucratic or punishing. Friendly, precise copy. Clear feedback at every step. The tone is like a knowledgeable colleague who speaks plainly and earns trust through competence, not formality.

## Anti-references

- **ServiceNow / legacy GRC tools**: heavy gray grids, overwhelming information density, dated form chrome, no visual hierarchy. The aesthetic of things nobody chose willingly.
- **Fintech dashboards (dark + neon)**: dark-mode drama with glowing metrics. Compliance is not a trading terminal. The mood should be calm and focused, not urgent and alarming.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over structure** — information hierarchy through typography and space, not boxes and dividers. The eye should land on what matters without hunting.
2. **Earned calm** — the interface should feel settled and reliable. Teal-anchored neutrals instead of enterprise gray. Confidence through restraint.
3. **Density with breathing room** — compliance officers work in long sessions with many fields. Handle information density gracefully; never feel cramped, never feel empty.
4. **Friendly precision** — human labels, plain-language status messages, helpful defaults. Precision in the data doesn't require sterility in the presentation.
5. **Trust through consistency** — predictable affordances, predictable patterns. When the stakes are real, unpredictable UI erodes confidence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Keyboard-navigable workflows (especially multi-step forms and approval flows). Color is never the sole carrier of meaning for status (risk level, approval state). Sufficient contrast on all interactive elements.
